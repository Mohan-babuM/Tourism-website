Favourite Places

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, is a stunning testament to the power of love and architectural brilliance. Located in Agra, India, this magnificent white marble mausoleum was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, known as the Harmandir Sahib, is a spiritual and architectural marvel situated in Amritsar, Punjab, India. This sacred Sikh shrine is renowned for its striking beauty and profound religious significance. The temple is adorned with an exquisite gilded facade, which gives it its iconic name, the "Golden Temple."

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a resplendent architectural gem and an iconic landmark located in the city of Mysore, in the southern Indian state of Karnataka. This magnificent palace stands as a testament to the opulence and grandeur of the Wadiyar dynasty, the former rulers of the princely state of Mysore.

Varanasi Temple

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is one of the oldest and holiest cities in India, renowned for its spiritual significance and the numerous temples that dot its landscape. Among these temples, the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, holds a special place of reverence. Located on the western bank of the sacred Ganges River, this temple is a hub of religious and cultural activities.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is considered to be the greatest architectural achievement in the whole range of Indo-Islamic architecture. Its recognised architectonic beauty has a rhythmic combination of solids and voids, concave and convex and light shadow; such as arches and domes further increases the aesthetic aspect. Not a piece of architecture, as other buildings are, but the proud passions of an emperor love wrought in living stones.
As the sun rises and sets, the Taj Mahal undergoes a mesmerizing transformation, bathed in shades of pink, gold, and silver. This exquisite play of light and shadow adds to the monument's ethereal beauty, making it a photographer's paradise and a source of inspiration for poets, artists, and lovers alike.
The Taj Mahal is not just a remarkable architectural marvel; it's also a poignant symbol of love and devotion. The story of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal's love, tragically cut short by her death during childbirth, is woven into its very fabric. Their love story lives on through the timeless beauty of this monument, a place where visitors from around the world come to pay their respects, marvel at its grandeur, and experience the overwhelming sense of serenity and romance that the Taj Mahal imparts.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib is a gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. There are many places to visit Near Golden Temple like Jallianwala Bagh, Wagah Border, Harike Wetland, Bathinda Fort.
The shimmering golden exterior of the temple reflects its spiritual significance, symbolizing the divine connection between humanity and the divine. The Golden Temple stands as a beacon of peace, unity, and devotion, attracting millions of pilgrims and tourists from all corners of the globe who come to experience its serene beauty and the spiritual aura that envelops this sacred place. It is not only a place of worship but a profound symbol of the Sikh faith and the values it represents.
One of the most unique and beautiful aspects of the Golden Temple is the continuous recitation of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, by a group of devoted individuals, accompanied by soul-stirring kirtan (devotional music). Visitors are welcomed with open arms, and the temple provides a free communal kitchen, or "langar," that serves meals to anyone, regardless of their background, emphasizing the principles of equality and service central to Sikh beliefs.

Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also called Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the most magnificent and largest palaces in India. Situated in the southern state of Karnataka, it used to be the official residence of the Wodeyar Dynasty, the rulers of Mysore from 1399 to 1950. The grand palace stands tall in the heart of Mysore city and attracts visitors from across the world. Being one of the prime attractions in India after the Taj Mahal, it certainly deserves a place in every traveler’s bucket list.
One of the palace's most striking features is its intricate and colorful facade, adorned with ornate carvings, frescoes, and stunning stained glass windows. The interior of Mysore Palace is equally breathtaking, with a series of grand halls, ornate ceilings, and exquisite artwork that reflects the opulence of the Wadiyar dynasty, the royal family of Mysore.
Mysore Palace stands as a testament to the rich cultural and architectural heritage of India and is a must-visit destination for tourists, history enthusiasts, and anyone who appreciates the splendor of royal palaces. It continues to be a symbol of the city's royal legacy and remains an integral part of Mysore's identity.

Varanasi Temple

Sri Kashi Vishwanath Temple also known as the Golden Temple, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the presiding deity of the city. Varanasi is Said to be the point at which the first jyotirlinga, the fiery pillar of light by which shiva manifested has supremacy over others gods, broke through the Earth’s crust and flared towards the heavens. More than the Gaths and even the Ganga, the Shivalinga installed in the temple remains the devotional focus of Varanasi.
The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is a marvel of architectural beauty, with its intricate spires and domes. Pilgrims and devotees flock to the temple throughout the year to seek blessings and to witness the powerful Aarti ceremony performed on the ghats of the Ganges. This nightly ritual is a breathtaking spectacle, as priests conduct a ritual offering to the sacred river while chanting hymns and ringing bells.
